House & Appliances

A few small things to keep the place happy for you and the next guests:

  • No smoking or vaping inside the condo or on the balcony. There's a designated smoking area by the grills, between the two condo buildings.
  • No pets — this is a strictly enforced pet-free building (HOA rule).
  • No luggage carts inside the condo — a strict HOA requirement.
  • Up to 6 guests, and the primary guest must be 25 or older. No parties, please.
  • Quiet hours are 10 PM to 10 AM (building policy) — sound carries between the units and over the water.
  • Pool hours are 9 AM to 10 PM.
  • Trash goes down the chute just outside the unit: turn left out the front door, and it's through the service door just past the elevators (you'll open the door yourself).

Appliance quick-notes:

  • Thermostat is at the end of the hallway, just before the kitchen — a Honeywell touchscreen. Use the up/down arrows to change the temperature, or tap Mode to switch between heat, cool, and off, then Done. Please switch the A/C and heat off whenever you leave the balcony door open.
  • Washer/dryer is the stacked unit in the shower area of the Jack & Jill bathroom; starter pods are provided.
⚠️
Heads up — the front door can slam: When the balcony slider is open, the draft can catch the front door and swing it shut hard. Hold the door as you open or close it, or close the slider first.
🏝️
Resort amenities: Your stay includes two Gulf-front pools, a fitness room, a tennis court, and BBQ grills between the buildings. The heated pool (seasonal, November–March) is at the back of the EAST building — your building. Reach the pool and beach through the back doors of the main lobby toward the parking garage; a gate at the back of the pool area opens to the beach walkover.

A few of these need an access code, all provided with your check-in instructions: the poolside restroom, the tennis court (in front of the building), and the fitness room (in the WEST building, off the main lobby).

Beach Service

Beach service is included March through October — two chairs and an umbrella set up for you daily.

  • Where: The beach attendant is stationed directly at the end of the walkover. Just let them know you're staying in unit 105.
  • If the attendant isn't there: Feel free to take any empty set of chairs that doesn't have a tag on the back or towels laid across it — the attendant will come by to ask which unit you're in.
  • What's included: Two chairs and one umbrella, set out each morning.
  • Your unit number: 105 — that's all the attendant needs.
🌊
Beach flags matter here: green is calm, yellow is caution, red means dangerous surf (knee deep is too deep), double red means the water is closed, and purple is a warning for dangerous marine life. Okaloosa Island flies them daily near the beach access — always check before you swim. Want daily flag updates by text? Text BEACH to 44144.
